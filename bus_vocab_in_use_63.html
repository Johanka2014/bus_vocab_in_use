Key Concepts

A: Preparing to Negotiate

Before starting, planning is crucial:

  • Information & Etiquette: Learn about the situation and culture (negotiating styles, acceptable behavior).
  • Priorities & Objectives: Decide your needs (what you must have) and objectives (goals).
  • Fallback Position: Conditions you will accept if your main objectives aren't met.
  • Venue: Choose the location:
    • On your own/home ground (your offices)
    • On their ground (their offices)
    • Neutral ground (e.g., a hotel)
  • Roles: If in a team, allocate responsibilities.

B: Negotiating Scenario

Typical steps at the start of a negotiation:

  1. Meet and greet: Introduce colleagues.
  2. Small talk: Offer coffee, create a relaxed atmosphere.
  3. Get down to business: Move to the meeting room.
  4. Agenda: Have a clear timetable.
  5. Background: State key facts and the situation.
  6. Kick off: Start discussing priorities and requirements.

C: Cultural Styles

Consider these factors when negotiating internationally:

• Body language • Physical contact • Conversational rules • Relationship building • Hierarchy • Attitude to time
