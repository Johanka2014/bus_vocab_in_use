Key Concepts

A: Types of Negotiation

Negotiations aim for mutual advantage (good for both sides) or to settle disputes.

Reaching Agreement

  • Customer-supplier: Buying/selling.
  • Wage: Pay discussions.
  • Merger/Takeover: Companies joining.
  • Trade: Between countries.

Settling Disputes

  • Contract disputes: Legal disagreements.
  • Labour disputes: Workers vs. management.
  • Trade disputes: Tariffs/bans.

B: Describing Negotiations

  • Intense / Intensive: Difficult, tiring, active.
  • Delicate / Tense: Difficult, could easily fail.
  • Eleventh-hour / Last-minute: Taking place at the very end of available time.
  • Protracted: Taking a very long time.

"He is a tough negotiator (good at getting what he wants)."

C: Bargaining

Bargaining is often used for discussing prices or pay (e.g., collective bargaining).

  • Bargaining ploy/tactic: A technique used to gain advantage.
  • Bargaining chip/tool: An issue used for leverage.
  • Bargaining point: An issue to be discussed.
  • Bargaining power: The strength of one side.
