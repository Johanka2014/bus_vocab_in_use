Key Concepts

A: Names

How names are used in business can reveal a lot about a company's culture. In the English-speaking world, it's common to use first names (e.g., Tom) even with new acquaintances. However, it's always safest to start with a formal title like Mr, Mrs, or Miss, plus the family name / surname (e.g., Mr Brewster). The title Ms is a neutral option for women. Remember, titles are never used with first names alone (e.g., "Mr Tom" is incorrect).

B: Business Cards

A business card typically includes your full name, job title (e.g., Sales Director, Senior Partner), and contact information. It's also a place to list professional qualifications or certifications after your name (e.g., B.S., C.P.A.).

C: Dress Code

Workplace attire varies widely. A business suit is standard formal wear. For less formal situations, a jacket and trousers that don't match is acceptable. Some companies adopt a smart casual look, where suits and ties are less common. A growing trend is casual Fridays or dress-down Fridays, allowing less formal clothing at the end of the week. Finally, many customer-facing roles require a uniform to ensure a consistent appearance.
