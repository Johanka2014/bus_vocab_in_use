Understanding Assets

An asset is something a company owns that has value or the power to earn money. They are categorized into three main types:

1. Current Assets

Assets that can be easily converted into cash within a year. Examples include:

  • Money in the bank
  • Investments
  • Money owed by customers (receivables)
  • Stock of goods to be sold (inventory)

2. Fixed Assets

Long-term assets that a company uses to produce goods or services and is not likely to be converted into cash within a year. Examples include:

  • Equipment & Machinery
  • Buildings
  • Land

3. Intangible Assets

Non-physical assets that still have value. Examples include:

  • Goodwill (a company's reputation)
  • Brands
  • Patents and trademarks
