Key Concepts

A: Win-Win & Probing

A successful negotiation leads to a win-win solution (equal benefit). One way to achieve this is probing (asking the right questions).

  • "What is the situation on...?"
  • "What sort of quantities are you looking for?"
  • "What did you have in mind regarding...?"
  • "What were you thinking of in terms of...?"

B: Proposals & Counter-Proposals

Use conditionals to make offers that depend on the other side's actions:

Common Phrases:

  • • "If you offer..., then we will/can..."
  • • "As long as..., then we could..."
  • • "On condition that..., then we might..."
  • • "Supposing that..., then we may..."
  • • "Provided/Providing that..., then we..."

C: Trade-offs & Bargaining

  • Concession: Offering to change your position to one less favorable to yourself (giving something up).
  • Goodwill gesture: A concession made hoping the other side will be friendly in return.
  • Trade-off: Giving something away to get something else in return.
  • Horse-trading: Making a series of concessions (often used to show disapproval).
  • Haggle: Arguing about something (usually price) for a long time.
