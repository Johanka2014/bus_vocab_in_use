Meetings 5: Discussion Techniques

Vocabulary Exercises

Key Concepts

A: Hedging

Hedging is when you avoid disagreeing directly to be polite or diplomatic. Common phrases include:

  • "I take your point about punctuality, but..."
  • "I understand what you're saying about..., but..."
  • "I see/know what you mean, but..."
  • "I hear where you're coming from on this, but..."

B: Controlling the Discussion

Interrupting Politely

  • "Can I come in here?"
  • "If I can just stop you for a moment..."
  • "Sorry to interrupt you, but..."

Checking Understanding

  • "Are you saying/suggesting that...?"
  • "Are you implying that...?"
  • "If I understand you correctly..."

Referring Back

"As we were saying earlier...", "To go back to what I was just saying..."

C: Outcomes & Conclusions

  • Consensus: Something most people can agree with.
  • Compromise: An agreement where people accept less than they originally wanted.
  • Agree to disagree: Accepting that a disagreement cannot be resolved.
  • Concluding phrases: "I'm afraid we're running out of time...", "To go over what's been said...", "I think that's it."
