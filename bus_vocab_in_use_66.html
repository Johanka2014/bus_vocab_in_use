Negotiations 5: Reaching Agreement

Vocabulary Exercises

Key Concepts

A: Deadlock and Resolution

When negotiations fail, they may break down. This leads to:

  • Deadlock / Stalemate / Impasse: A situation where no progress can be made.
  • Irreconcilable differences: Differences that cannot be fixed.

Solutions:

  • Mediator: An outsider who helps sides reach an agreement (Mediation).
  • Arbitrator: An outsider (like a judge) who imposes a settlement or resolution (Arbitration).
  • Cooling-off period: A time where each side takes no action.

B: Agreements and Contracts

An agreement is a deal or bargain. You can clinch or close a deal.

Contract Types

  • Employment: About a job/employees.
  • Commercial: Buying or selling a product.
  • Oral / Verbal: Not written down.
  • Binding / Legal: Forces both sides to act by law.

C: Checking the Deal

Useful phrases for summarizing and finalizing:

  • "Let me just run over (summarize) the main points."
  • "As far as [X] is concerned..."
  • "We still have the question of [X] to settle."
  • "There is still the outstanding (remaining) issue of..."
  • "We'll draw up (write) a contract."
