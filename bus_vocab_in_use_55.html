Meetings 2: The Role of the Chairperson

Vocabulary Exercises

Key Concepts

A: Before the Meeting

A good chairperson is a good organizer. Preparation is key.

  • The Chairperson: The chairperson is responsible for presiding over the committee meetings .
  • The Agenda: The list of things to be discussed.
  • To Distribute: To give a share or a unit of (something) to each of a number of recipients.
  • The Venue: The room or place where the meeting happens. The chair must check it is free and without interruptions.

B: During the Meeting

  • The chair must be a good timekeeper
  • Start on time; don't wait for latecomers.
  • Appoint a minute-taker: Someone to take the minutes (notes), recording opinions and action points (tasks participants agree to do).
  • Allocate time: Ensure each point gets the time it deserves and keep to the timetable.
  • Move on: Go to the next point when time is up, even if the issue isn't fully resolved (decided).
  • Tactfully: Deal with disagreements diplomatically. Ensure everyone can make their point.
  • Avoid digressions: Stop people from getting off the point.
  • Ensure the meeting finishes on time.

C: Follow-up

  • Circulate minutes: Send them out, especially if there are action points that particular people are responsible for.
  • Next meeting: Check if the minutes are an accurate record of what happened.
  • Matters arising: Discuss points from the last meeting that need further attention.
