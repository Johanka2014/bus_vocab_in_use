Key Concepts

A: Confrontation

When negotiations become confrontational or adversarial (fighting each other):

  • Bargaining Power: Negotiating from strength (strong position) vs. negotiating from weakness.
  • Demands: Objectives that are non-negotiable (will not be changed). You might "moderate" (reduce) demands later.
  • Under duress: Being forced to accept something you don't want.
  • Giving in: When one side accepts the other's demands, they have conceded, backed down, or climbed down.

B: Difficult Tactics

Tricks used by tough negotiators:

  • Ultimatums / Threats: "Do this, or else..."
  • Bluffing: Threatening to do something you don't actually intend to do.
  • Calling someone's bluff: Pretending to believe them when you know they are lying, forcing them to act.

C: Dealing with Problems

When negotiations get stuck:

  • Underline common ground: Focus on what is already agreed.
  • Reassure: Confirm key points haven't changed.
  • Compromise: Accept less than original objectives.
  • Identify sticking points: Find the exact obstacles.
  • Postpone discussions: Take a break to reconsider positions.
