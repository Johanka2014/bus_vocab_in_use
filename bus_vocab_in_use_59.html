Key Concepts

A: Types of Presentation

  • Press conference: Executives speak to journalists (e.g., about a merger).
  • Briefing: Senior officer gives info to colleagues (e.g., police operation).
  • Demonstration: Showing how something works (e.g., a new machine).
  • Product launch: Announcing a new product (e.g., car model).
  • Lecture: Professor communicating to students.
  • Talk: Informal presentation to a group (e.g., club members).
  • Seminar: Expert giving advice to a small group (e.g., investments).
  • Workshop: Practical session where people practice (e.g., yoga).

B: Dos and Don'ts

Tips for a stand-up presentation:

  • Audience: Find out who they are and how much they know.
  • Venue: Check the room, seating plan, and equipment.
  • Content: Plan structure but don't write the complete text.
  • Notes: Write notes on sheets of paper, not on cards (to avoid dropping them).
  • Memorize: Learn the first five sentences by heart to settle nerves.
  • Rehearse: Practice with friends until it becomes familiar.

C: Introduction Phrases

  • Introduce yourself: "My name's... and I work for..."
  • Outline the talk: "There are three main skills areas I want to talk about today..."
  • Handle questions: "If you have any questions, I'll be very happy to answer them at the end."
