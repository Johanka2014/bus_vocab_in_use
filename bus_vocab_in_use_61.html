Vocabulary Exercises

Key Concepts

A: Timing (Dos and Don'ts)

  • Start on time: Don't wait for latecomers.
  • Plan timings: Decide how long to spend on each point.
  • Don't labour a point: Don't spend too long on one thing.
  • Don't digress: Avoid talking about things unrelated to the subject.
  • Finish on time: Don't "run over" (go past the time limit).

B: Voice and Rapport

Using Your Voice

  • Project your voice (don't shout).
  • Check the volume beforehand.
  • Don't speak in a monotone.
  • Vary the pitch (level) of your voice.

Building Rapport

  • Tell an anecdote (story).
  • Mention a surprising fact.
  • State a problem.
  • Ask a question.

C: Key Phrases

Useful phrases for structuring the main body of your talk:

  • "To begin, let's look at..."
  • "Related to [X] is..."
  • "But I'm digressing: let's get back to..."
  • "That's all I have time for on [X]. Let's move on to..."
  • "As you can see on this transparency/slide..."
  • "I think that covers everything on..."
