Vocabulary Exercises

Key Concepts

A: Opening the Meeting

There are several standard ways to start a meeting and state its purpose:

  • Getting started:

    • "Let's get down to business."

    "Shall we make a start?"

    "Let's begin, shall we?"

    "It's about time we got started."

  • Stating purpose:

    • "As you know / As you are aware..."

    "I've called/arranged this meeting to..."

    "The main objective/purpose is to..."

B: Inviting People to Speak

To encourage participation, you can use these phrases:

  • To start the discussion:

    "Would you like to open the discussion / kick off?"

    "Perhaps you'd like to get the ball rolling."

  • Asking for specific opinion:

    • "What about you?"

    "What are your feelings/views on this?"

    "What do you think?"

  • Asking the group: "What's the general feeling on this?"

C: Making Your Point

When expressing opinions, clarity is key. Note that "Of course" and "Obviously" imply others should already know the fact, so use them carefully to avoid sounding rude.

    "I believe..."

    "In my opinion..."

    "Personally, I think..."

    "The way I see it..."

    "As I see it..."

    "It's clear to me that..."

    "It looks to me as if..."
