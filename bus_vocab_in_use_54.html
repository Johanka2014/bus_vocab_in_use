Meetings 1: Types of Meeting

Vocabulary Exercises

Key Concepts

A: Word Combinations with 'Meeting'

  • arrange / set up / fix: To organize a meeting.
  • bring forward: To make a meeting earlier than planned.
  • put back / postpone: To make a meeting later than planned.
  • cancel: To decide not to have a meeting.
  • run / chair: To be in charge of a meeting.
  • attend: To go to a meeting.
  • miss: To not go to a meeting.

B: Types of Meeting

  • chat: An informal discussion.
  • brainstorming: A meeting to produce as many ideas as possible.
  • project/team meeting: A meeting for employees on a specific activity.
  • departmental meeting: A meeting for a specific department.
  • meeting with suppliers/customers: To negotiate prices or discuss contracts.
  • board meeting: A formal meeting of a company's directors.
  • AGM (Annual General Meeting): Where shareholders discuss the annual report.
  • EGM (Extraordinary General Meeting): A special shareholders' meeting for an important issue (e.g., a merger).

C: How was the meeting?

  • Positive: productive, useful discussions, reached an agreement, covered a lot of ground, got through a lot, kept things moving, stick to the point, achieved a lot.
  • Negative: a waste of time, digressions, rambling, wandering off the point, show off.
