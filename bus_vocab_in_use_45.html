Key Concepts

A: Cultures and Culture

Culture is "the way we do things here." This can refer to a country, a region, or an organization. Key terms include:

  • Company or corporate culture: The way a company works and its values.
  • Canteen culture: Unofficial ways of thinking and talking within an organization, not approved by leadership.
  • Long-hours culture: The expectation that employees work long days.
  • Macho culture: Ideas associated with men, like aggressiveness and strength.

Be careful of stereotypes: fixed ideas that may not be true.

B: Distance and Familiarity

The relationship between managers and employees varies across cultures. Some are informal, where managers are accessible and approachable. Others are more formal, where managers are distant and remote, and employees show a lot of deference (accepting decisions without participating). This often relates to how hierarchical a company is, meaning how many management layers it has.

Exercise 45.2: Company Comparison

Read about the two companies and answer the questions.

Two Box Companies

The Associated Box Company (ABC) and the Superior Box Corporation (SBC) both make cardboard boxes.

At ABC there are three levels of management between the CEO and the factory workers. At SBC, there is only one.

Managers at ABC are very distant. They rarely leave their offices, use an executive restaurant, and are hardly ever seen by employees. Employees are never consulted. At SBC, managers share the same canteen and have long meetings with employees before taking important decisions.

SBC has an open-door policy where any employee can see a manager. At ABC, employees must solve problems only with their immediate manager.

At ABC, employees call managers 'sir'. At SBC, everyone uses first names.
